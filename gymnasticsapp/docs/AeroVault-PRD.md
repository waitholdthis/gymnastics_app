# AeroVault Product Requirements & Visual Design Strategy

## Executive Summary
AeroVault turns gymnastics progress tracking into a cinematic quest where young athletes chase podium milestones while parents get calm, trustworthy insight into growth, safety, and competition readiness. It combines skill tracking, flexibility metrics, score history, privacy-first video tools, and coach/org workflows into a premium training companion built to be credible enough for USA Gymnastics partnership conversations.

## Elevator Pitch
AeroVault is the mission-control app for the modern gymnastics family: kids unlock their Path to Podium while parents and coaches see progress, safety signals, and highlight-worthy moments in one trusted place. It makes training feel like a quest, not homework.

## Visual Style Guide

### Brand Vibe: Cinematic Professionalism
AeroVault should feel like a high-end training facility inside a blockbuster interface: deep navy command surfaces, Olympic gold achievement states, chalk-white typography, electric cyan motion cues, and small magenta/coral moments for kid energy. The interface should be premium, athletic, and fast, but never intimidating.

### Dynamic Motion Design
Menus behave like responsive training equipment. As a child swipes through the Path to Podium, nodes tilt subtly, apparatus cards compress then spring open, and the background reacts with parallax rings that imply arena lights and chalk dust. Parent dashboards avoid noisy motion; they use slower panel reveals, gentle progress bars, and confidence-building confirmations.

### Cinematic Elements
High-quality slow-motion video is integrated into every major skill page as an “Epic Moment” strip: attempt, coach cue, best frame, and final replay. A lightweight 3D avatar mirrors the gymnast’s progression, earning new warm-up jackets, chalk textures, and podium poses as skills move from working to mastered to competition-ready. “Epic Moment” replays auto-detect sticks, salutes, first-time completions, and score PRs, then create shareable clips with cinematic lower-thirds.

## Kid Experience

### Path to Podium
The skill checklist becomes a quest map organized by apparatus worlds: Vault Launchpad, Bars Orbit, Beam Skyline, and Floor Arena. Each skill is a node with three states: Training, Mastered, and Podium Ready. Nodes unlock side quests for flexibility, strength, confidence, and recovery so the child learns that preparation is part of mastery.

### Virtual Chalk
Kids earn Aero-Coins, badge plates, chalk streaks, and cinematic title cards. Rewards symbolize effort and mastery rather than only scores: “Brave Beam Reset,” “Stick Landing,” “Consistency Streak,” and “Coach Cue Champion.” Parents can choose reward intensity by age so a 7-year-old gets delight and a 16-year-old gets a cleaner athlete profile.

### AR Form Overlay
Video capture can open an AR “Perfect Lines” mode. The app overlays reference shoulder, hip, knee, and ankle alignment guides on top of practice video, then shows a split-screen comparison against coach-approved form. The child sees friendly visual cues like “tight hollow,” “open shoulders,” and “landing chest up,” while parents see only simple takeaways.

## Parent Experience

### Scout Dashboard
The parent view avoids technical overload by translating training data into plain-language cards: Control Growth, Readiness, Recovery Risk, Confidence Trend, and Competition Exposure. Parents can tap into detail, but the default view answers three questions: Is my child improving, are they healthy, and what should we ask the coach next?

### Fort Knox Safety & Privacy
Child profiles and videos default to private. Sharing requires guardian approval, expiring links, watermarking, download controls, and granular audience choices: family, coach, club, scout, or public highlight. Sensitive data such as age, location, injury notes, and full routines are protected behind role-based access, audit logs, encryption in transit and at rest, and clear consent records.

### Safe Highlight Reels
Parents can generate auto-edited Highlight Reels with cinematic music, meet title cards, score overlays, and privacy-safe cropping. Before export, AeroVault checks for visible names, location boards, and other children in frame, then suggests blurring or trimming. Reels can be shared publicly only after guardian confirmation.

## Strategic Growth

### USA Gymnastics Appeal
AeroVault becomes valuable to clubs and organizations by standardizing progress language, reducing parent confusion, and producing trustworthy development records. The org version can support sanctioned skill libraries, coach-verified milestones, athlete safety reporting, and aggregate anonymous participation insights.

### National Ranking & Talent ID
The Talent ID layer is opt-in and permission-gated. Athletes can submit coach-verified profiles to regional or national discovery pools with limited data views: age band, level, apparatus strengths, verified scores, consistency trends, and approved highlight clips. Scouts never see private health notes or unapproved media.

### Pro-Tips
Olympic athlete masterclasses appear inside the exact skill node a child is training. Instead of a separate video library, the Kip node might include a 45-second elite cue, a slow-motion body-shape breakdown, and a kid-friendly challenge. Coaches can pin or hide masterclasses to match their training philosophy.

## The Wow Factor
### “Arena Replay”
Arena Replay is a never-before-seen cinematic feature that turns a child’s best routine moment into a broadcast-quality mini scene. The parent selects a clip, and AeroVault detects the key beat: takeoff, flight, landing, salute, or score reveal. The app then creates a 12-second replay with slow motion, subtle arena-light treatment, a glowing form-line overlay, apparatus label, skill name, and a final “Quest Cleared” badge animation. For kids, it feels like being the hero of the meet. For parents, it becomes a safe, beautiful memory worth keeping and sharing.
